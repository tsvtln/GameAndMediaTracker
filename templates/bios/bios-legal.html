BIOS Legal Information

What is a BIOS?

A BIOS (Basic Input/Output System) is firmware originally stored inside a video game console.
It contains essential system code required to start the hardware and load games.
Some emulators require the original BIOS to properly reproduce the behavior of the real console.

Legal Ownership of BIOS Files

BIOS files are copyrighted software owned by the console manufacturer. In most countries, distributing or downloading BIOS files without permission from the copyright holder may violate copyright law.

Generally, BIOS files should only be used if you legally own the original hardware and have created a personal backup from your own device.

Personal Backups

Some users choose to create their own BIOS backups from hardware they own. Laws regarding personal backups vary by country and jurisdiction.

It is the responsibility of the user to ensure that their use of BIOS files complies with the laws in their region.

Educational and Preservation Purposes

Many BIOS files are used by developers, researchers, and enthusiasts for purposes such as:

  • studying historical gaming hardware
  • software preservation
  • emulator development
  • compatibility testing

These activities help preserve the history of video game systems and digital media.
